# Chapter 1: Web Novelist Cyberbullied

The morning sunlight struggled to pierce through the gaps in the curtains, a sliver of light falling into the bedroom on the twenty-second floor of a high-rise building.

The curtains were purple, and the sunlight outside made the purple even more vibrant, making the air in the bedroom look as if it were dyed a faint shade of purple.

The bedroom was decorated simply and elegantly. Qianli Xun was lying on the bed, snoring soundly. Her messy hair was scattered on the pillow, and the quilt was pulled up to her neck, letting out a gentle snoring sound.

The phone ringtone on the bedside table rang, clearly a popular song of the moment.

Qianli Xun didn't open her eyes, her hand reaching for the bedside table. After feeling around for a while, she finally grabbed the phone, still without opening her eyes, and asked in a voice heavy with sleep, "Hello, what's it?"

On the other end of the phone came the anxious voice of her apprentice, Ergang: "Master! Why are you still sleeping? Quickly open your phone and watch the video I sent you. Everyone on the internet is cursing you!"

Hearing this, Qianli Xun was completely awake. She sat up: "What? Everyone on the internet is cursing me? Why?"

"See for yourself! I told you a long time ago not to write web novels, but you just wouldn't listen. Hide at home and don't come out lately. Hanging up!"

Qianli Xun sat up, and the quilt naturally slid off her upper body, revealing light purple pajamas. She hurried to open the chat tool on her phone and found Ergang's avatar, which was the god of wealth; this guy dreamed of hitting it big every day. After clicking on it, she indeed saw a video link. She hurriedly opened it, and a woman's voice came out of the video:

"Web novelist Qianli Xun has been reported by many netizens for plagiarism, self-plagiarism, and padding her latest works. She has lost the spirit of her early works and is purely formulaic, failing to capture the interest of readers..."

Qianli Xun frowned and closed the video, not wanting to listen any further. She cursed, "Yours truly has been dominating the webnovel world for ten years! How I write is none of your business!"

She threw her phone aside, got out of bed, put on her slippers, walked into the kitchen, and took a bottle of milk from the fridge, pouring half a glass.

The milk was very cold since it just came out of the fridge, and drinking it cold isn't good for the body—Qianli Xun knew that much. She was too lazy to heat it up, so she just picked up the thermos next to her and added some hot water to the glass. The temperature was just right. She drank it down in big gulps, then slammed the glass heavily onto the table out of anger.

She crossed the living room, walked onto the balcony, and looked downstairs. Many male and female reporters were blocking the entrance, constantly looking up. Fortunately, she lived in a high-end community with security in every building; those reporters couldn't get up at all.

A smile appeared at the corner of Qianli Xun's mouth: "I didn't expect I was this famous, so many reporters came. Well, someone who can be cyberbullied is at least a minor celebrity."

She left the balcony and went back into the bedroom, reaching for the phone on the bed. Sitting on the edge of the bed, she took a deep breath: "Witness how famous you are with a relaxed mind."

She opened a short video app, and the first video she swiped to was吐槽ing her. It was a female streamer, quite ordinary-looking, speaking in a measured voice:

"Hello everyone! The name Qianli Xun isn't unfamiliar, right? Yes! She's the famous web novelist, having written several novels that I bet many of you have read. To be honest, I've read them too, and I used to be her fan. Note that I said 'used to.' Why not now? Because as a reader, I've grown, but she, as a writer, is still standing still! She's still writing things that eighteen or nineteen-year-old girls like to read. How can she not be complained about by fans? Looking back at those old novels, I finally said to myself: The past you was really stupid, pure, and naive, otherwise how could you like such stories?..."

Qianli Xun felt her inner strength was great enough to have listened to so much, but she still couldn't help but retort to the screen: "What do you know! Yours truly targets readers of that age group! You liked reading them in your teens, doesn't that prove yours truly writes well? It's just that you're no longer young!"

While complaining, a message popped up. Qianli Xun closed the video and opened the message—to her surprise, more than half of her fans had left the group.

"Big deal! So what if they leave? Yours truly is a writer, not a star. When the hosting site asked to create a fan group, yours truly was very reluctant. It's cleaner now that they've left. Just leave the whole group!"

Just as she finished speaking, another message arrived, notifying her that the number of people in the fan group had dropped to single digits. Qianli Xun didn't bother and swiped away the livestreamer. What met her eyes was a bold title: "Literature is Dead." This title piqued her interest. She thought, *This one surely has nothing to do with me, might as well listen.*

An old man appeared in the video, looking quite scholarly, his voice booming and aggressive:

"Literature is dead! It's a tragedy! A lament! The literary works of traditional writers are left unread, while young people prefer to read those nutrient-free web novels on their phones! Literature should serve reality! It should expose reality, criticize reality! Literature is for conveying the Truth! Web novels, for economic benefit, blindly cater to the market and the readers—it's entertainment to death! 'Face-slapping' stories and novice works are everywhere! Not a single one meets the requirements of literature! Take Qianli Xun's novels for example..."

All that lead-up was still just to criticize Qianli Xun. It seemed she had become notorious overnight. She would listen to how this aggressive old man criticized her.

"The whole internet is criticizing her, so I went to look at Qianli Xun's novels. I read a bit and couldn't go on. It's all trash, no substance at all! Traditional literary language has depth and rich content; it's worth savoring. Web novels simply cannot compare! I call on everyone! While the whole internet is criticizing Qianli Xun, let's start a wave of criticism against web novels and revive traditional literature!..."

Qianli Xun tapped the screen, and the old man's voice stopped immediately. She pointed at the phone screen:

*"You speak so passionately, write a web novel yourself if you're so capable. You definitely can't! Literature isn't dead! In the internet age, literature has taken on a new appearance to meet readers. Don't you understand literary history? If not, go read it. Every era's literature has its own unique characteristics. Traditional literature no longer fits this era and will inevitably be phased out. Your desire for revival is useless! Who knows, in a few hundred years, my, Qianli Xun's, works might also become classics. Yours truly also wants to ask you, what's wrong with my novels making readers happy? Cao Xueqin once said that 'Dream of the Red Chamber' was just 'penciled for play and to suit one's temperament.' Even the greatest of the Four Great Classical Novels entertained the masses; web novels are simply inheriting literature's fine traditions!..."*

Before Qianli Xun could finish venting, the words "Editor Youyou" flashed on the screen. It was a call from her editor.

Qianli Xun tapped the green button, and a gentle female voice came through: "Qianli Xun, the site's top management has decided that due to the excessive number of online trolls, in order to maintain the site's image, all your works will be taken down, including the one you're currently writing. The site will compensate you appropriately for the losses caused by the removal."

"How can they do this? Besides, being criticized shows I have fame, so my works shouldn't be taken down..."

The editor didn't want to listen any further and quickly hung up. Qianli Xun was both helpless and angry. She wanted to throw the phone on the floor to vent her frustration but held back. She had to think carefully about what to do next. Was her creative career over just like that? This was what she loved doing most.
