## Chapter 2: Duel, Earthquake, and Transmigration

Before she could even figure out her next move, her stomach growled in protest. That single glass of watered-down milk was a total joke. Qianli Xun marched back to the dining room and yanked the fridge open. Aside from the milk, it was completely barren.

She furiously dialed her apprentice's number. "Hey, Er Gang. Your master is officially getting canceled online. I'm trapped—there's a whole mob of bloodthirsty reporters swarming downstairs. Go fetch me a box of rice noodle rolls, a cup of soy milk, and a bowl of spicy tofu pudding. And make damn sure they drown that tofu pudding in chili!"

"That's a lot," Er Gang questioned dubiously. "Can you even finish all that?"

"The whole internet is cursing me out. Shouldn't I eat my fill to deal with them?" Qianli Xun retorted sharply. "Oh, right. When you come over, bring me an ancient-style women's outfit. And don't forget the veiled hat."

"What do you need those for?"

"I'm heading back to Qianli Fist Manor. With so many reporters practically camping downstairs, I need a disguise so they don't jump me."

"It's a good thing everyone only knows you as a web novel author. No one has a clue about your connection to Qianli Fist Manor," Er Gang noted. "Alright then, just head back to the manor and lay low. Wait up, I'll be there soon."

About half an hour later, Er Gang arrived downstairs on his rumbling motorcycle. Juggling her breakfast and a large paper bag, he threw a dismissive glance at the reporters, strode into the building, and whispered something to the security guard. He then ducked toward a side elevator. The doors slid open a moment later, and he stepped inside, punching the button for the 22nd floor.

The reporters didn't pay Er Gang the slightest attention. Clad in a black martial arts uniform that showcased his bulging, muscular arms, he didn't look like he belonged in Qianli Xun's world at all.

Stepping off at the 22nd floor, Er Gang knocked on his master's door. Once inside, he tossed the bulky paper bag onto the sofa, walked straight into the kitchen, and set the breakfast out on the dining table.

He grumbled the entire time. "You really shouldn't have been writing novels, Master. Your status as the 508th generation successor of Qianli Fist is more than enough for you to flaunt! But now look—you're getting canceled by the whole damn internet. Oh well. From now on, you can just barricade yourself in the manor and take on some commercial performances. You'll make enough to live comfortably, marry yourself off, and call it a perfect life."

"I am the master here! Since when is it an apprentice's turn to lecture his master?" Following closely behind him, Qianli Xun sat down at the table, ripped open the plastic bag, and eagerly dug into her breakfast.

"Take your time eating, Master," Er Gang sighed. "I'll wait for you outside the complex."

Qianli Xun ignored him, happily picking up a piece of rice noodle roll with her chopsticks. Hearing the front door click shut behind him, she muttered bitterly, "These apprentices of mine take me less and less seriously. Just wait until I get back to the manor—I am *definitely* going to discipline them."

Qianli Xun truly had a ravenous appetite. She polished off every last bite of the breakfast, wiped her mouth, and lobbed the crumpled tissue into the trash can. Stretching lazily, she sauntered into the bathroom. She quickly brushed her teeth, washed her face, and smoothed out her flowing long hair.

Refreshed, she returned to the living room and tore open the paper bag. Shedding her pajamas, she changed into the new outfit. 
*Not bad,* she thought, admiring herself. She looked exactly like an ancient swordswoman. Stashing her phone and valuables into her bag, she slung it securely across her body. Finally, she donned the veiled hat. The light gauze draped perfectly over her face—it would be nearly impossible to recognize her now.

She slipped into a pair of casual shoes at the entryway, opened the door, and rode the elevator down. When the doors dinged open on the first floor, an inexplicable wave of anxiety gripped her. As she crept past the security guard, he was furiously swiping through short videos on his phone. Judging by the loud, dramatic audio, the videos were blasting Qianli Xun yet again. Utterly engrossed in the gossip, the guard didn't even glance at the woman walking out.

Qianli Xun stepped through the lobby doors. Through the translucent veil, she immediately noticed the reporters whipping their heads in her direction. Her anxiety spiked.

A female reporter aggressively approached her. "Hello there! Excuse me, which floor of this building do you live on?"

"The 22nd floor," she blurted out.

Hearing '22nd floor,' the reporters instantly swarmed her like sharks smelling blood. "You live on the same floor as Qianli Xun? Do you know anything about her situation?"

Qianli Xun instantly regretted her honesty. She could only blame the sheer panic for making her IQ violently plummet.

"I don't know her very well. All I know is she lives opposite my unit," she lied with a remarkably straight face. "Early this morning, I heard miserable crying coming from her place. Probably frightened out of her wits by all the cyberbullying. Anyway, keep up the good work. I have places to be."

Frantically pushing her way through the mob, Qianli Xun practically sprinted to the complex gates. *"Thank God,"* she muttered to herself, exhaling sharply. *"They didn't recognize me."*

"Master! Over here!" Er Gang's booming voice rang out.

Qianli Xun rushed over, swung her leg over the back of the motorcycle, and wrapped her arms tight around Er Gang's waist. *Vroom!* Accompanied by the roaring engine, they sped down the road, heading straight for Qianli Fist Manor.

---

Qianli Fist Manor was nestled in the city's sprawling suburbs. It boasted over a hundred apprentices, with both male and female disciples fiercely training within its walls.

When the motorcycle finally screeched to a halt outside the manor, Shen Jianchen—impeccably dressed in a tailored suit and polished leather shoes—was already there. He had once again brought his Shen Fist disciples to face off outside the main gates. Tensions were high, but it looked like the two sides hadn't actually traded blows yet.

Qianli Xun yanked off her veiled hat, tossed it into Er Gang's arms, and shouted, "Shen Jianchen! Aren't we supposed to duel on the 25th of every month? Why the hell are you here early?"

Shen Jianchen turned slowly. Seeing Qianli Xun dressed as a fierce swordswoman, looking exceptionally beautiful, the corners of his deep eyes crinkled. He let out a low, velvety chuckle. "I woke up early this morning and saw you become the internet's number one enemy. I rushed over to check on you."

Er Gang casually hung the veiled hat on the motorcycle, swaggered up to Shen Jianchen, and smirked. "You're the CEO of a massive corporation. Leaving your empire unattended just to bring your disciples to challenge us every month... could it be that you've got a thing for my master?"

Shen Jianchen's gaze remained fixed intensely on Qianli Xun. "It's true that I have a thing for your master, but the duel is also real. You apprentices should spend some time talking some sense into her. She's already an old maid hitting thirty. If she doesn't get married soon, absolutely no one is going to want her."

After a morning of relentless cyberbullying with nowhere to vent her fury, being called "old" was the absolute final straw. 

"Yours truly is only turning thirty tomorrow!" Qianli Xun exploded, her eyes blazing.

Shen Jianchen chuckled softly. "Stop calling yourself 'yours truly' all day long. You're already getting up there in years; constantly saying that only makes you sound even older."

Qianli Xun's fury hit critical mass. "Shen Jianchen, you listen to me! Yours truly is going to duel you to the death today! If you lose, you are permanently banned from Qianli Fist Manor!"

"And what if you lose?" Shen Jianchen challenged, raising an eyebrow.

"If I lose, I lose! What are you going to do about it?" Qianli Xun sneered. "Besides, yours truly will *never* lose!"

"If you lose, you marry me," Shen Jianchen declared smoothly.

Hearing this bold claim, the Qianli Fist disciples started furiously whispering among themselves. "This CEO Shen is flawless in every way, except he has zero clue how to court a girl. He's pissing Master off to the extreme—how is she ever going to agree to marry him?"

"In your dreams!" Qianli Xun snapped back. "When pigs fly, yours truly *might* reconsider marrying you! Tonight at seven. By the Millennial Divine Willow. Come alone. You bring anyone else, and it's off!"

"Understood!" Shen Jianchen looked entirely too pleased with himself. He turned to his disciples and ordered briskly, "Retreat!"

The Shen Fist disciples scrambled after their master toward a sleek Rolls-Royce. Noticing the constant, irritating smile plastered on his face, one disciple eventually couldn't hold back. "Master, you're about to get beaten to a pulp. Why are you so happy?"

"What do you mean 'beaten to a pulp'?" Shen Jianchen scoffed lightly. "This is clearly a date! Tonight, when the bright moon rises by the Millennial Divine Willow... it reminds me of a poem." 

Shen Jianchen beamed, not looking worried in the slightest:

> [POEM]
> *When the moon ascends the willow's crest,*
> *Lovers rendezvous at twilight's rest.*
> [TN: A famous quote from the Song dynasty poet Ouyang Xiu, capturing the romantic essence of an evening rendezvous.]

The disciples helplessly watched him slide into the elegant car, murmuring among themselves. "With a brain like his, how did Master ever become a CEO? How does he manage a corporate empire? Who let him drive a car that luxurious?"

---

At dusk, beneath the Millennial Divine Willow in the quiet suburbs, Qianli Xun stood waiting. Her hair was tied back in a high, practical ponytail. Dressed in a simple white T-shirt, casual jeans, and running shoes, she looked brilliantly youthful—not at all like a woman turning thirty the very next day.

Shen Jianchen had arrived in casual wear. He looked relaxed, effortlessly handsome, and stared at Qianli Xun with eyes absolutely brimming with affection.

The rhythmic croaking of frogs from the nearby pond echoed in waves, breaking the heavy, expectant silence between them.

Once the noisy frogs finally died down, Shen Jianchen glanced up at the moon. It was nearing the fifteenth of the lunar month, and the pale orb hanging in the sky was approaching perfect fullness. Looking back at the massive, ancient Divine Willow bordering the pond, he chuckled. "Hey, Qianli Xun! If you wanted to go on a date with me, you could have just said so. Why use a duel as an excuse?"

"Which ear of yours heard that I wanted to date you?" Qianli Xun sneered coldly. "Yours truly is here to beat you down!"

Before he could respond, Qianli Xun lunged forward like an arrow released from a bow, hurling a devastating punch straight at his jaw.

Shen Jianchen evaded swiftly, slipping to the side.

Unwilling to yield an inch, Qianli Xun intensified her offense. She launched a ferocious assault, chaining together a vicious, lightning-fast combination of strikes to completely suppress him.

Shen Jianchen dodged and weaved, parrying and deflecting her rapid-fire blows, smoothly neutralizing her aggressive offense with softer martial techniques. However, seeing Qianli Xun unleash a terrifying flurry of rapid punches, he immediately abandoned his defense, channeled raw power into his own fists, and met her head-on. 

*Bam!* 

When their fists collided, the sheer concussive force sent both of them staggering backward over two meters.

"Qianli Xun, why do you push yourself so hard as a girl?" Shen Jianchen sighed, regaining his balance. "Why don't you just marry me? I'm an incredible catch. Not only am I the 360th generation successor of Shen Fist, but I'm also the CEO of a major corporation. You wouldn't find someone like me even if you searched with a lantern in broad daylight. So many women want to marry me, yet I'm exclusively interested in you. After you marry me, you won't need to write those web novels anymore. I'll take care of you."

Perhaps because he had softly referred to her as a "girl," Qianli Xun's foul mood unexpectedly lifted. She even felt a tiny, irritating flutter of emotion. Subconsciously, though, she remained as verbally unrelenting as ever. "Don't even dream about it. I told you, unless pigs can fly, yours truly will *never* marry you. And I am a woman of my word."

Right as the words left her mouth, the ground beneath their feet suddenly began to heave violently. 

The amplitude of the tremors was so monstrous that both of them instantly lost their footing, crashing hard into the dirt. Frozen in shock, they watched the distant nearby buildings crumble into dust and rubble in the blink of an eye. In that terrifying instant, they finally realized a massive earthquake had struck. It was a brutal reminder of just how fragile humans were in the face of nature's wrath.

Above them, the Millennial Divine Willow swayed violently. The ancient roots tore free from the churning earth, and the colossal tree began to tilt—plummeting directly toward Qianli Xun.

Spotting the shadow falling over her, Shen Jianchen desperately scrambled across the upending ground. He violently threw his body over hers just as the massive Divine Willow crashed down, completely crushing them beneath its unfathomable weight. Warm blood instantly seeped into the cracked, violent soil.

About ten minutes later, a haunting stillness fell over the area. The entire landscape had been reduced to a desolate wasteland. 

Suddenly, Qianli Xun felt a strange pulling sensation. She was peeling away from her physical body, floating weightlessly into the cold air. Looking down, she saw her own mangled corpse pinned brutally beneath the Divine Willow. A wave of agonizing pain and sorrow washed over her.

Then, she saw Shen Jianchen's ethereal form peeling away from his corpse as well. 

"Shen Jianchen! Shen Jianchen!" she screamed desperately at the top of her lungs, but he couldn't hear a sound.

As Shen Jianchen floated higher, their eyes finally met. Qianli Xun could clearly see he was screaming her name in return. She frantically tried to respond, but their voices remained eternally silenced to one another.

Suddenly, an overwhelming surge of divine power erupted from the thick, ancient trunk of the shattered Millennial Divine Willow. The sheer force gripped the two floating souls, sweeping them violently away. Qianli Xun only felt her vision plunge into absolute, terrifying darkness. 

She was utterly terrified...
